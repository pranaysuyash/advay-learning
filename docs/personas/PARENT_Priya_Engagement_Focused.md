# Persona Interview: Priya (Engagement-Focused Parent, Age 33)

## Overview

**Interview Date:** 2026-02-25  
**Persona:** Priya — Engagement-Focused Parent (Non-teacher)  
**Location:** Pune, Maharashtra  
**Children:** Rohan (5y 1m)  
**Occupation:** Operations Manager (non-education)  
**Primary Concern:** “I just need to know he was engaged and safe — not graded.”  
**Current Status:** Uses app 3–4 evenings/week for 15–20 minutes

---

## Interview Transcript

### Opening

**Interviewer:** Priya, when you check a learning app, what makes you feel it’s worth using?

**Priya:** I don’t want grades. He’s five. I want to know he was engaged, and what he did. If I see a simple summary — “played 15 minutes, did tracing and drawing” — I’m happy.

**Interviewer:** What about something like “0/26 mastered”?

**Priya:** That sounds like school. It makes me worry I’m behind. I’d rather see progress in a gentle way — like “explored 5 letters.”

---

### Progress Expectations

**Interviewer:** How often would you check progress?

**Priya:** Weekly is fine. If it’s daily, I won’t look. A Sunday summary I can glance at is perfect.

**Finding #1: Wants Weekly, Lightweight Progress**

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Evidence:** Prefers a “Sunday summary” over daily dashboards
- **Impact:** Daily stats will be ignored; weekly summaries are more likely to be read
- **Recommendation:** Weekly “Recess Report” with 3–4 bullets and time totals

---

### Communication & Trust

**Interviewer:** What makes you trust a school or app?

**Priya:** Clear communication. I want to see what they did, not just a trophy icon. If the summary feels honest and simple, I trust it.

**Finding #2: Clear, Simple Communication Builds Trust**

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Evidence:** Explicitly ties trust to clarity and transparency
- **Impact:** Overly gamified “stars-only” reports reduce trust
- **Recommendation:** Use simple language (e.g., “explored,” “played,” “tried”) and avoid grade-like metrics

---

### Parent Value Signals

**Interviewer:** What would make you pay for it?

**Priya:** If it gives me a calm, safe break and I can show my husband “here’s what he did.” That’s the value. Not school marks.

**Finding #3: Value = Safe Engagement + Shareable Summary**

- **Severity:** MEDIUM-HIGH
- **Evidence:** Will pay if it provides a reliable break plus a simple summary to share
- **Impact:** Progress should be framed as engagement evidence, not achievement
- **Recommendation:** Add a parent-shareable weekly summary (WhatsApp-friendly)

---

### Peer Comparison

**Interviewer:** Do you want to compare your child to others?

**Priya:** No. That makes me anxious. If there’s a competition feature, it should be optional. I’d rather focus on his own progress.

**Finding #4: Avoid Forced Peer Comparison**

- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Evidence:** Explicit discomfort with comparisons
- **Impact:** Default leaderboards could reduce parent trust
- **Recommendation:** Make comparisons opt-in and default to “self-progress” views

---
