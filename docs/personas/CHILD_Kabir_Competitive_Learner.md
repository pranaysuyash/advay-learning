# Persona Interview: Kabir (The Competitive Learner, Age 7)

## Overview

**Interview Date:** 2026-02-24  
**Persona:** Kabir — The Competitive Learner (Child, 7y 3m)  
**Location:** Hyderabad, Telangana  
**Context:** Grade 2 student, CBSE board, son of Vikram (Data-Driven Father)  
**Primary Concern:** "This is baby stuff" — biggest churn risk in upper age range  
**Current Status:** Has been using app for 3 months, getting bored

---

## Interview Transcript

### Opening

**Interviewer:** Kabir, thanks for talking to me! You've been using this app for a while. What do you think?

**Kabir:** [Shrugs] It's okay, I guess. But I've done all the levels. It's... [pauses]... kind of for babies now.

**Interviewer:** For babies? Tell me more about that.

**Kabir:** [Opens app, shows Alphabet Tracing] See? I trace A, B, C. I've done this like 100 times. The mascot dances and says "Great job!" but... [makes a face]... I'm in Grade 2. I know my letters. This is for my sister [Meera, age 4].

---

### Age-Appropriate Content Gap

**Interviewer:** What would you want to learn instead?

**Kabir:** [Gets excited] Cursive! My teacher says we'll learn cursive writing next term. But I want to start now. Also... [thinks]... bigger words. Not just "cat" and "dog." Words like "dinosaur" and "extraordinary." 

And math! Real math, not just counting. Like... [counts on fingers]... 47 + 28. My dad gives me problems like that. The app only goes up to 10. That's baby math.

**Finding #1: No Upper Age Content (6-8 Years)**
- **Severity:** CRITICAL (Churn Risk)
- **Evidence:** Kabir has "done all levels," content stops at Grade 1 level
- **Impact:** 7-8 year olds churn immediately when content runs out; they'll tell parents "this is boring"
- **Recommendation:**
  - Add Grade 2-3 content: Cursive writing, 2-digit arithmetic, complex words
  - "Next Level" unlock system visible to children
  - Age-appropriate themes: Space, dinosaurs, robots, sports (not "baby animals")
  - Difficulty scaling: Current "Hard" should be the new "Easy" for 7y+

---

### Motivation & Competition

**Interviewer:** What makes you want to keep playing a game?

**Kabir:** [Thinks hard] Beating my score! Like in Subway Surfers — I try to beat my high score every time. Or like chess with Papa — I want to win.

This app... [opens progress screen]... it gives me stars. But what do I do with them? [Shows star count: 1,247] I have so many. They don't mean anything.

I want to know: Am I better than other kids my age? Like, am I in the top 10%? My dad always asks "Is this app making you smarter than your friends?" I don't know what to tell him.

**Finding #2: No Competitive Progression System**
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Evidence:** Stars accumulate without purpose; no peer comparison; no skill-based ranking
- **Impact:** No motivation loop for competitive learners; external rewards lose value
- **Recommendation:**
  - **Percentile ranking:** "You're faster than 78% of 7-year-olds"
  - **Skill tiers:** Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum → Diamond (like games he plays)
  - **Weekly challenges:** "This week: Trace 100 letters in under 5 minutes"
  - **Streak system:** "7-day streak! Keep going!"
  - **Spendable currency:** Stars buy avatar items, themes, badges (not just accumulate)

---

### UI/UX Feedback

**Interviewer:** What about how the app looks? 

**Kabir:** [Makes a face] The mascot is... okay. But it's like for little kids. [Points at Pip] Look at the big eyes and the silly dance. My friends would laugh if they saw me using this.

I want something cooler. Like... [opens YouTube]... see this Minecraft skin? Dark colors, looks powerful. Or like Roblox — I can make my own character. Can I make my own mascot? Like, choose a robot or a dragon instead of... [points at Pip]... a blob thing?

**Finding #3: UI Perceived as "Babyish" for Upper Age Range**
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Evidence:** Bright colors, dancing mascot, simple animations don't appeal to 7y+
- **Impact:** Social embarrassment; won't use in front of friends; asks parents for "big kid" apps
- **Recommendation:**
  - **Age-adaptive UI:** 6-8 year olds get "Big Kid Mode" with:
    - Darker/muted color scheme option
    - Less "cute" mascot animations
    - More "cool" themes: Space, tech, superheroes
  - **Character customization:** Choose avatar (robot, ninja, astronaut)
  - **Remove childish language:** "Amazing!" → "Solid!" or "Nice!"
  - **Faster animations:** No slow bouncy effects

---

### Social Features

**Interviewer:** Would you want to play with your friends?

**Kabir:** YES! Like, can I challenge Arjun [classmate]? Like: "I bet I can trace faster than you!" And we both do it and see who wins. That would be cool.

Or like... [thinks]... a leaderboard in my class. Who's the best at math? I want to be #1. My dad says I'm good at math. I want to prove it.

But... [looks concerned]... not everyone should see my scores. Just my friends. And don't tell people if I'm bad at something. That's embarrassing.

**Finding #4: Missing Social/Competitive Features**
- **Severity:** MEDIUM-HIGH
- **Evidence:** Wants peer challenges, class leaderboards, friend competitions
- **Impact:** Solo play feels isolating; no social proof or validation
- **Recommendation:**
  - **Friend challenges:** "Challenge Arjun to a tracing race!"
  - **Class leaderboards:** Anonymous rankings (show "Player #3" not names for privacy)
  - **Head-to-head mode:** Two kids play simultaneously, see who finishes first
  - **Privacy controls:** Opt-in to share scores; hide struggling areas
  - **Team challenges:** "Your class vs. other schools" (aggregated, anonymous)

---

### Parent Interaction

**Interviewer:** What does your dad think about the app?

**Kabir:** [Frowns] He asks me every week "Did you learn something new?" And I have to say... [sighs]... "I practiced letters." But I already KNOW letters! He looks disappointed.

I want to show him I'm actually learning. Like... [excited]... "Papa, I learned cursive G today!" Or "I can do 56 + 37 now!" Something he can test me on and see I'm getting better.

The app sends him... [checks phone]... "Kabir earned 50 stars this week!" He doesn't care about stars. He wants to know if I'm smarter than last month.

**Finding #5: Progress Not Visible to Parents as "Real Learning"**
- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Impact:** Parent disappointment → subscription cancellation (Vikram's churn risk)
- **Evidence:** Kabir can't demonstrate tangible new skills; dad unimpressed by stars
- **Recommendation:**
  - **"New Skills Unlocked" notification:** "This week: Cursive A-G mastered!"
  - **Parent quiz mode:** "Test Kabir on these new words he learned"
  - **Grade-level advancement:** "Completed Grade 1 content → Starting Grade 2"
  - **Real-world skill mapping:** "Can now write full sentences" (not just "traced 100 letters")

---

### Content Depth

**Interviewer:** If you could design your perfect learning app, what would it have?

**Kabir:** [Very excited now]

1. **Stories I read:** Not just "The cat sat." Real stories! Like Harry Potter but for my age. And I have to read them, not just tap.

2. **Science!** I want to learn about black holes and robots. The app has... [checks]... "Connect the Dots." That's not science.

3. **Languages:** My Nani speaks Telugu. I want to learn Telugu letters too. Not just English and Hindi.

4. **Projects:** Like, build a rocket in the app. Step by step. Collect parts. Then launch it! That's cool.

5. **Tournament mode:** Weekly tournament. Everyone plays the same challenge. Winner gets... [thinks]... a special badge or something.

**Finding #6: Content Lacks Depth and Real-World Connection**
- **Severity:** MEDIUM-HIGH
- **Evidence:** Wants science, multilingual, projects, stories — not just basic skills
- **Impact:** Sees app as "baby practice" not "real learning"
- **Recommendation:**
  - **Multilingual expansion:** Telugu, Tamil, Kannada content (connect to heritage)
  - **Science modules:** Simple physics, space, nature (age-appropriate)
  - **Project-based learning:** "Build a robot" over 5 sessions
  - **Interactive stories:** Reading comprehension with choices
  - **Weekly tournaments:** Same challenge, global participation

---

### Frustration Points

**Interviewer:** What annoys you about the app?

**Kabir:** [Counts on fingers]

1. **Too slow.** The mascot takes forever to finish talking. I just skip it.

2. **Can't skip easy stuff.** I have to do A-B-C every time before I get to harder letters. Let me start where I want!

3. **No dark mode.** My eyes hurt at night. I play before sleeping.

4. **Crashes.** Sometimes it just stops. Then I lose my progress. [Angry face]

5. **Baby sounds.** [Makes squeaky voice] "You did it!" Ugh. I'm 7, not 2.

**Finding #7: UX Friction Points for Older Children**
- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Evidence:** Forced progression, slow animations, no dark mode, crashes
- **Impact:** Frustration → reduced usage → churn
- **Recommendation:**
  - **Unlock any level:** "You're advanced — jump to Grade 2 content!"
  - **Fast-forward option:** Skip mascot animations
  - **Dark mode:** Essential for older kids (they use devices independently)
  - **Crash recovery:** Auto-save progress every 30 seconds
  - **Age-appropriate feedback:** "Solid!" instead of squeaky "Yay!"
