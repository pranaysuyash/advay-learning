# Persona Interview: Mira (Curious Explorer, Age 4)

## Overview

**Interview Date:** 2026-02-25  
**Persona:** Mira — Curious Explorer (Child, 4y 7m)  
**Location:** Bengaluru, Karnataka  
**Context:** Preschooler, uses tablet with parent nearby  
**Primary Concern:** Wants to “tap everything” and gets upset by locked content  
**Current Status:** Short, high-curiosity sessions (5–8 minutes)

---

## Interview Transcript (Observed + Simulated Responses)

### First Interaction

**Interviewer:** Mira, what do you want to do first?

**Mira:** [Points at screen] That one! [Taps multiple tiles quickly]

**Interviewer:** What if it says “locked”?

**Mira:** [Frowns] Why no? I want this. [Taps again, then turns away]

**Finding #1: Locked Content Triggers Frustration**

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Evidence:** Repeatedly taps locked items, disengages quickly
- **Impact:** Gating causes early drop-off in younger children
- **Recommendation:** Remove hard locks for 4–5 age band; use optional challenges instead

---

### Exploration Behavior

**Interviewer:** Do you want to finish one game or try many?

**Mira:** Many! [Switches between letters and drawing]

**Finding #2: Exploration > Completion**

- **Severity:** HIGH
- **Evidence:** Prefers quick switching and novelty
- **Impact:** Mastery-based progression doesn’t match 4–5 attention patterns
- **Recommendation:** Track “explored” and “tried” rather than “mastered”

---

### Rewards & Feedback

**Interviewer:** Do you like stars?

**Mira:** Yes! More stars! [Smiles]

**Interviewer:** What about “Level 2 locked”?

**Mira:** [Shrugs] I want the stars. The lock is “no.”

**Finding #3: Rewards Work, Locks Don’t**

- **Severity:** MEDIUM-HIGH
- **Evidence:** Responds to simple rewards but ignores/resents locks
- **Impact:** Light extrinsic feedback helps, but gating punishes curiosity
- **Recommendation:** Keep stars as celebration, remove lock messages

---

### Parent Interaction

**Parent (observing):** If she gets stuck, she just quits. She doesn’t try to “unlock.”

**Finding #4: Unlocks Don’t Motivate at 4–5**

- **Severity:** MEDIUM
- **Evidence:** Parent confirms disengagement when blocked
- **Impact:** Unlock mechanics are ineffective for preschoolers
- **Recommendation:** Replace unlocks with “suggested next” cards and voice prompts
