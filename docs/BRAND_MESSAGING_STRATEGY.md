# Advay Vision: Brand Messaging & Copywriting Strategy

**Date:** 2026-02-21
**Purpose:** Defining the core messaging pillars, taglines, and copywriting rules for the Advay platform (Parent-Facing) and Pip (Child-Facing).

---

## 1. The Core Messaging Pillars
Every piece of copy must align with one of these three pillars:

### Pillar 1: "Digital Magic, Physical Reality"
*   **The Idea:** Kids use their real hands, real body, real expressions, and real voice. We don't just track fingers; we track jumping, dancing, and smiling.
*   **Keywords:** Full-Body, Active, Camera-first, Pose, Face, Voice, Real-world.

### Pillar 2: "Explore the Physical World, Safely" (Risk-Free Reality)
*   **The Idea:** Allowing kids to do everything they want to do in the physical world (mixing explosive chemicals, painting the walls, building giant towers) without the danger, the mess, or the clean-up.
*   **Keywords:** Safe, virtual-physical, mess-free, explore, experiment.
*   **The Idea:** Kids don't fail, they just find new ways to play. The engine adapts silently.
*   **Keywords:** Explore, Playground, Earn, Discover, Frustration-free.

### Pillar 3: "Worry-Free for Parents"
*   **The Idea:** Private, ad-free, and guaranteed to stop when the child is tired or the parent leaves the room.
*   **Keywords:** Safe, Private, On-device, COPPA, AI-monitored.

## 3. Copywriting Rules: Parent-Facing (The "Advay" Voice)

**Archetype:** The Empowering Educator
**Tone:** Smart, Professional, Reassuring, Transparent.

#### The Golden Rules
1.  **Skip the guilt-trip:** Never talk down to parents about "too much screen time." Framing should be positive ("Make screen time active").
2.  **Jargon-free Tech:** Don't say "We use MediaPipe Hand Landmarker." Say "Our camera tracks their fingers in real-time."
3.  **Privacy is Paramount:** Remind them constantly that video never leaves the device.

#### UI Copy Cheat Sheet (Parents)
*   Instead of "Buy Subscription", say "Unlock the Full Playground"
*   Instead of "Screen Time Limit Met", say "Pip Needs a Nap"
*   Instead of "Age 5", say "Level: The Deep Forest"

## 4. Copywriting Rules: Child-Facing (The "Pip" Voice)

**Archetype:** The Cheerful Companion
**Tone:** Energetic, Concrete, Funny, Encouraging.

#### The Golden Rules
1.  **Verbs First:** Kids respond to action. Start sentences with *Show me, Wave, Tap, Jump, Grab.*
2.  **Concrete Feedback:** Don't say "Good job on that math problem." Say "Wow! You caught 5 red apples!"
3.  **Failure must be funny:** Never use "Wrong." "Oops! The tower went crash! Let's build a stronger one!"
4.  **Audio over Text:** Assume the child cannot read. Every piece of UI copy must be accompanied by a Pip voiceover.

#### UI Copy Cheat Sheet (Kids)
*   **Loading Screen:** "Waking up Pip..." -> "Pip is stretching!" -> "Ready to play!"
*   **Error (Camera blocked):** "Uh oh! I can't see your hands! Are they hiding?"
*   **Success (Stars earned):** "Three bright stars! You're a superstar!"
*   **Frustration Detected:** "This is a tricky puzzle! Take a deep breath with me... ready?"

**Hero Section**
*   **Headline:** Turn Screen Time into Active Play.
*   **Subheadline:** The first educational app where kids use their hands, bodies, and voices to control the game. No extra hardware required.
*   **CTA:** Start Your 7-Day Free Trial.

**Feature Section 1: The Magic of Camera Play**
*   **Headline:** Put down the mouse. Use your hands.
*   **Body:** Advay's secure camera tracking lets your child solve math by holding up fingers, learn phonics by tracing in the air, and explore science through safe, virtual experiments.

**Feature Section 2: The Open Playground**
*   **Headline:** No strict levels. Just endless exploration.
*   **Body:** Kids learn best when they lead. Our AI silently adjusts the difficulty of every game in real-time, ensuring they are always challenged, but never frustrated.

*   **Headline:** What happens in the room, stays in the room.
