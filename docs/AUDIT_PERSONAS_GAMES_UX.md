# Personas for Games UX Audit (TCK-20260205-001)

## Child Learner Personas

### Persona 1: Asha (2-3 years old)

**Profile**: Toddler, beginning learner, short attention span
**Motivations**:

- Bright colors and sounds
- Immediate feedback/rewards
- Simple cause-effect interactions

**Pain Points**:

- Long instructions overwhelm
- Complex gestures frustrate
- Needs parent guidance
- Gets bored fast

**Expected Behaviors in Audit**:

- Random clicking/tapping
- Can't follow written instructions
- Distracted by visual noise
- Needs adult supervision

**Questions to Answer**:

- Are buttons large enough for small hands?
- Is feedback immediate and celebratory?
- Can game be played without reading?
- Does UI avoid sensory overload?

---

### Persona 2: Dev (4-6 years old)

**Profile**: Preschooler, can follow simple instructions, learns through play
**Motivations**:

- Achievable challenges with rewards
- Progress tracking (stars, badges)
- Friendly guidance and encouragement
- Variety and new content

**Pain Points**:

- Complex rules confuse
- Failure discourages
- Wants to win quickly
- Gets frustrated with hard controls

**Expected Behaviors in Audit**:

- Follows step-by-step instructions
- Tries multiple times on failure
- Gets excited about rewards
- Wants to know "What's next?"

**Questions to Answer**:

- Are instructions visual + voice?
- Do games have appropriate difficulty for age?
- Is progress visible (stars, level badge)?
- Are error messages encouraging vs discouraging?

---

### Persona 3: Maya (7-9 years old)

**Profile**: Early elementary, logical thinker, wants challenge and depth
**Motivations**:

- Meaningful progression ("Master the alphabet!")
- Competition/leaderboards
- Problem-solving challenges
- Achievement unlocks

**Pain Points**:

- Too easy = boring
- Too hard = frustrating
- Wants to understand "why"
- Doesn't want to feel patronized

**Expected Behaviors in Audit**:

- Reads and follows complex instructions
- Tries to optimize performance
- Asks "What's the hardest level?"
- Wants to progress to harder games

**Questions to Answer**:

- Is there difficulty progression?
- Are hard modes available?
- Is learning outcome clear?
- Does UI feel age-appropriate (not babyish)?

## Parent/Guardian Personas

### Persona 4: Priya (Parent of 2-3yr)

**Profile**: Concerned parent, wants safe learning, limited screen time
**Motivations**:

- Child safety and privacy
- Quality of learning
- Ease of supervision
- Daily guidance

**Pain Points**:

- Privacy concerns with camera
- Can't tell if child is learning
- Ads/external links scary
- Tech should be simple

**Expected Behaviors in Audit**:

- Reads privacy policy
- Watches child during session
- Checks dashboard for progress
- Worried about camera usage

**Questions to Answer**:

- Is privacy policy clear and accessible?
- Can parent set session time limits?
- Is there progress feedback?
- Is there emergency exit/pause?

---

### Persona 5: Rajesh (Parent of 4-6yr)

**Profile**: Engaged parent, wants balanced learning, tracks progress
**Motivations**:

- Understand child learning objectives
- See measurable progress
- Age-appropriate challenge
- Teacher-friendly reports

**Pain Points**:

- Can't see "did my kid learn?"
- No comparison/benchmarking
- Wants to involve teacher
- Concerned about screen addiction

**Expected Behaviors in Audit**:

- Checks progress dashboard regularly
- Wants to print progress report
- Asks child "What did you learn?"
- Shares achievements with family

**Questions to Answer**:

- Is progress dashboard complete and accurate?
- Can parent see specific skills mastered?
- Are there weekly/monthly summaries?
- Can progress be shared/exported?

## Audit Strategy

**Total Personas**: 8 (can expand to 12+ from FRESH_35_PERSONA_AUDIT_2026-02-05.md if needed)

**Testing Approach**:

- Each game tested with 3-4 most relevant personas
- Child personas: Full gameplay playtest (5-10 min each)
- Parent personas: Dashboard + settings review
- Educator: Teacher mode setup (if exists)
- Designer: Full UX audit + engagement assessment

**Evidence Capture per Persona**:

- Screenshots (key UX moments, errors, confusion points)
- Console logs (errors, analytics events firing)
- Behavior notes (confusion, delight, frustration, engagement)
- Time-on-task measurements
- Network logs (analytics data transmission)

**Expected Output**:

- 30-50 screenshots across all games
- 5-10 behavioral insights per game
- 3-5 critical findings per persona group
- Mapping of findings to specific files/components
- Remediation ticket recommendations with priority levels
