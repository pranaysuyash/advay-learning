## Codex Findings

These are my own repo-grounded conclusions added on top of the captured backlog.

### Highest-Leverage Near-Term Categories

For this codebase right now, the strongest next categories are:

1. **Exploration sandboxes**
   - Physics Playground
   - Sink or Float
   - States of Matter
   - Simple Machines

2. **Life-skill simulations parents immediately value**
   - Brush Teeth with Pip
   - Wash Hands Dance
   - Shoelace Simulator
   - Dress for Weather
   - Packing the School Bag

3. **Movement games with strong replay value**
   - Obstacle Course
   - Follow the Leader
   - Musical Statues
   - Balloon Pop Fitness

4. **Creative open-ended modes**
   - Sound Garden
   - Light Painter
   - Comic Book Creator
   - Drawings Come Alive

### Strongest Fit With The Vision

The best-fit ideas are the ones that:

- let the child explore without a fixed right answer
- turn body movement into play, not just assessment
- create visible cause-and-effect quickly
- stay fun even without scores or curriculum language

That makes:

- physics / sandbox play
- creative music / art modes
- pretend-play life skills
- body-based social play

better near-term bets than overly quiz-like fact games.

### Best Batch Shape For Future Work

The next good implementation batch should mix:

- 1 exploration-heavy sandbox
- 1 parent-valued life skill
- 1 fast-replay movement game

That gives better product learning than shipping three similar drill games together.

### Backlog Cleanup Note

Some items in this backlog overlap or could be unified later:

- `Shadow Puppets` vs `Shadow Puppet Studio`
- `Magic Wand` vs `Magic Wand (BLE)`
- multiple music-conductor / band-director style ideas

Before implementation, those should be deduplicated into clear product lanes instead of treated as separate guaranteed builds.
