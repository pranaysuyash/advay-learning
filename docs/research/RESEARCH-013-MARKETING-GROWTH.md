## 2. Product Hunt Launch Strategy

### 2.1 The 2025-2026 Reality

Product Hunt algorithm changed significantly:
- Only **10% of launches get featured** (down from ~30%)
- First **2-4 hours** are critical for momentum
- **Community engagement** before launch matters
- **Product quality** matters more than launch day tactics

### 2.2 Pre-Launch Checklist (4-6 Weeks Before)

```markdown
## Product Hunt Prep

### Week -6 to -4: Build Community
- [ ] Create Product Hunt account (if not exist)
- [ ] Engage on other launches (upvote, comment thoughtfully)
- [ ] Follow makers in EdTech/kids space
- [ ] Join Product Hunt Discord/community

### Week -3 to -2: Prepare Assets
- [ ] Create 1200x630 thumbnail (main image)
- [ ] Record 30-60 second demo video (no sound required)
- [ ] Write compelling tagline (max 60 chars)
- [ ] Write description (why this matters)
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 gallery images
- [ ] Create Maker intro video (optional, high impact)

### Week -1: Final Prep
- [ ] Schedule launch for Tuesday-Thursday, 12:01 AM PST
- [ ] Prepare launch day social posts
- [ ] Email warm contacts (don't ask for upvotes explicitly)
- [ ] Prepare FAQ for comments
- [ ] Test all links and app access

### Launch Day
- [ ] Post "We're live!" on social media
- [ ] Respond to EVERY comment within 1 hour
- [ ] Share behind-the-scenes on Twitter/X
- [ ] Thank supporters individually
- [ ] Monitor for questions/issues
```

## 4. Parent Community Engagement

### 4.1 Target Communities

| Community Type | Examples | Approach |
|----------------|----------|----------|
| School WhatsApp groups | Parent groups | Be helpful first, subtle mentions |
| Parenting Facebook groups | BabyCenter India, etc. | Answer questions, share expertise |
| Reddit | r/IndianParents, r/Parenting | Genuine participation, occasional plug |
| Mommy/Daddy bloggers | Indian parent bloggers | Offer free access for review |
| Preschool partnerships | Local preschools | Demo for teachers, parent nights |

### 4.2 Community Guidelines

```markdown
## DO:
- Provide value first, always
- Answer questions genuinely (even if not about your app)
- Share your parenting struggles and wins
- Be a community member, not a marketer
- Only mention app when genuinely relevant

## DON'T:
- Spam groups with app links
- Post promotional content repeatedly
- Fake reviews or testimonials
- Pay for fake engagement
- Ignore community rules
```
